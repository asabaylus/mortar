Now there is only one. (Technically, yes, Vatican City is a country.) The Saudi women in the photo gallery below voted today, for the first time in the nation’s 83-year history. Each of these women, it is safe to assume, cast her ballot for herself. The sole Saudi offices decided by public vote are seats on local councils, and for the 2015 elections, following a promise made by the late King Abdullah bin Abdulaziz, Saudi women have been permitted both to vote for their council members and to run as candidates—as long as they follow the rules.
